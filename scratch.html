Why I Hike

There's nothing like time out in nature to just be present and take in the world around you. I like that it makes me feel small, because then I remember that my problems aren't as big as I think they are. There's a whole world out there and that's what I like to focus on.

Utah is one of the most spectacular places on earth to explore on foot. There are slot canyons and alpine lakes, and every trail tells a different story.

Top Utah Hikes

  1. Angel's Landing — Zion National Park
    • 5.4 miles round trip
    • Difficulty: Strenuous
    • Best season: Spring & Fall
    • Iconic chain-assisted summit scramble
  2. Delicate Arch — Arches National Park
    • 3 miles round trip
    • Difficulty: Moderate
    • Best season: Spring & Fall
    • Utah's most iconic natural landmark
  3. Emerald Lakes — Uinta Mountains
    • 6.5 miles round trip
    • Difficulty: Moderate
    • Best season: Summer
    • Three stunning alpine lakes
  4. Peek-a-Boo Loop — Bryce Canyon
    • 5.5 miles round trip
    • Difficulty: Moderate–Strenuous
    • Best season: Spring & Fall
    • Weaves through surreal hoodoo formations
  5. Mount Timpanogos
    • 17 miles round trip
    • Difficulty: Very Strenuous
    • Best season: Late Summer
    • The crown jewel of Utah County
